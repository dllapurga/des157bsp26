Capstone Project Proposal

Topic Brief

My capstone project is an interactive website that serves as a virtual recipe archive, allowing people to preserve and share recipes passed down through their grandparents and families. At its core, the project explores how food functions not only as nourishment, but also as a living connection to culture, memory, and identity.

As the child and grandchild of immigrants in America, I have grown up with access to the flavors and dishes that shaped my family’s history. When I crave these foods, there are still relatives who know how to prepare them. However, I often think about future generations of my family—third, fourth, or even fifth generation descendants— who may no longer have that same cultural connection.

While cultural foods can still be found in restaurants, they can never fully replicate the intimacy, history, care, and generational knowledge embedded in family recipes. Food is one of the most enduring expressions of cultural identity, yet recipes are fragile forms of knowledge. Many are never formally written down and instead survive through memory, routine, and shared experience.

As generations pass, these culinary traditions risk disappearing, taking with them the stories, emotions, and histories tied to them. This project aims to preserve those recipes before they are lost to time, creating a digital space where future generations can reconnect with the flavors, memories, and cultural legacies of their families.

Design Lens

I believe the lens most applicable to my project is speculative design. The project imagines a future problem: the gradual loss of cultural memory and family recipes across generations of immigrants and diasporic families.

Rather than simply creating a recipe website, the project acts as a speculative response to cultural erasure by asking: What happens when family knowledge disappears? How can digital spaces preserve intangible heritage?

Through this lens, the project uses design to explore emotional, cultural, and intergenerational futures.

Features

  • Browse a virtual recipe archive
  • Add recipes through a form input
  • Include dish name, ingredients, and preparation steps
  • Navigate recipes through a circular scrolling interaction
  • Write and view memories associated with each dish
  • Upload family or food photographs
  • Ideal feature: upload a photo and use AI masking/upscaling to create consistent recipe thumbnails

Challenges

One challenge I foresee is creating the circular scrolling interaction. I believe it may involve experimenting with positioning, animation, and JavaScript logic, though I am still exploring the technical process. I am also trying to decide how to sort the dishes by culture because I come from a multicultural background or whether to sort them by cooking(meals)/baking(desserts)

Another challenge is implementing the AI-assisted image masking feature. Ideally, users would upload an aerial photograph of their dish, and the system would automatically isolate the plate and transform it into a uniform recipe thumbnail.

Since creating a fully functional AI system is beyond the scope of my current abilities, I am considering ways to simulate the interaction or visually suggest the effect without requiring actual machine learning.
